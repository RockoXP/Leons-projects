Professional Course in Data Sciences & AI Machine Learning

During my time at Le Wagon's Data Science & AI Machine Learning course, I had the privilege of participating in a collaborative predictive machine learning model project focused on forecasting house prices in various regions of Portugal. This hands-on experience encompassed the full data science pipeline, from data gathering to deploying a user-friendly application. Data Gathering and Preprocessing As a team, we worked together to source relevant data, ensuring it provided sufficient coverage of key variables like location, property size, and pricing trends. Cleaning and processing this data was a meticulous process, involving the handling of missing values, the removal of duplicates, and applying feature engineering techniques to enhance the dataset's predictive potential. My attention to detail and strong analytical skills were instrumental in identifying and addressing potential data quality issues. Model Selection and Development We explored multiple machine learning algorithms to identify the best-performing model for our task. Techniques such as linear regression, random forests, and gradient boosting were tested using cross-validation to ensure robust results. My contributions included not only helping evaluate the models but also interpreting the results to fine-tune the final model for optimal accuracy. Application Backend and Frontend The backend of our project was developed in Python, leveraging powerful libraries like Pandas for data manipulation, Scikit-learn for modeling, and Matplotlib/Seaborn for exploratory data analysis. For the frontend, we used Streamlit to create an intuitive and interactive web application, allowing users to input parameters and visualize predictions effortlessly. Innovative Features and My Contributions I took the initiative to enhance the application with several analytical and user-friendly features: Geospatial Map: I integrated a dynamic geospatial map that visualized house price distributions across Portugal, offering users insights into pricing patterns in specific regions. Heatmap and Correlation Analysis: I added a heatmap and a correlation graph that demonstrated the relationship between location and property size, highlighting key factors influencing price trends. Mortgage Calculator: To increase the app's practical utility, I implemented a mortgage calculator, enabling users to estimate monthly payments based on predicted house prices, interest rates, and loan terms. Chatbot Integration: To further improve user experience, our team developed a chatbot that answered users' questions about the dataset, the prediction model, and property-related queries. This feature made the app more interactive and accessible. Key Takeaways This project underscored the importance of teamwork and cross-disciplinary collaboration in delivering a comprehensive data product. I shined in bringing a strong analytical perspective and creative solutions, contributing to both the technical and functional aspects of the application. By combining data science with user-centric design, we created a tool that was not only technically sound but also practical and engaging for users. This experience solidified my passion for leveraging data and technology to solve real-world problems, further fueling my drive to innovate and contribute meaningfully in the tech space.
